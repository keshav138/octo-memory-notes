### Core Concepts

- Work = Time × Efficiency
- If A can complete work in 'n' days, A's one day work = 1/n
- If A and B together can do work in 'n' days, (A+B)'s one day work = 1/n

### Important Formulas

- **Combined Work**: If A does work in 'a' days and B in 'b' days, together they take: (a×b)/(a+b) days
- **Efficiency**: If A is twice as efficient as B, A takes half the time B takes
- **Work ratio = Efficiency ratio = Inverse of time ratio**

### Pipes and Cisterns

- Inlet pipes fill (positive work)
- Outlet pipes empty (negative work)
- Net work = Work by inlets - Work by outlets

### Wages Distribution

Wages are distributed in ratio of work done = Ratio of (Efficiency × Time)

### Practice Problems

**Example 1**: A can do work in 15 days, B in 20 days. Together in how many days? **Solution**: (15×20)/(15+20) = 300/35 = 60/7 ≈ 8.57 days

**Example 2**: A and B can do work in 12 days. A alone in 20 days. How many days will B take alone? **Solution**: B's one day work = 1/12 - 1/20 = (5-3)/60 = 2/60 = 1/30 B takes 30 days
