## TYPE 1: Basic Average Calculations

**Q1.** Find the average of 12, 15, 18, 21, 24, 27, 30.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

**Method 1 (Standard):** Sum = 12+15+18+21+24+27+30 = 147 Average = 147/7 = 21

**Method 2 (Shortcut for AP):** Average of arithmetic progression = (First term + Last term)/2 = (12 + 30)/2 = 21

**Answer: 21**

</details>

**Q2.** The average of 5 numbers is 27. If one number is excluded, the average becomes 25. Find the excluded number.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Sum of 5 numbers = 27 × 5 = 135 Sum of 4 numbers = 25 × 4 = 100 Excluded number = 135 - 100 = 35

**Answer: 35**

</details>

**Q3.** Find the average of first 50 natural numbers.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

**Formula:** Average of first n natural numbers = (n+1)/2 = (50+1)/2 = 51/2 = 25.5

**Answer: 25.5**

</details>

## TYPE 2: When New Elements Join/Leave

**Q4.** The average age of 30 students is 15 years. If the teacher's age (45 years) is included, what is the new average?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Total age of 30 students = 30 × 15 = 450 Total age including teacher = 450 + 45 = 495 New average = 495/31 = 15.97 years

**Quick Method:** Increase in average = (45 - 15)/31 = 30/31 ≈ 0.97 New average = 15 + 0.97 = 15.97

**Answer: 15.97 years**

</details>

**Q5.** Average of 10 numbers is 40. If two numbers 45 and 55 are removed, what is the new average?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Sum of 10 numbers = 10 × 40 = 400 Sum removed = 45 + 55 = 100 Remaining sum = 400 - 100 = 300 New average = 300/8 = 37.5

**Answer: 37.5**

</details>

**Q6.** The average of 20 numbers is 12. If three new numbers with average 16 are added, what is the new overall average?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Sum of 20 numbers = 20 × 12 = 240 Sum of 3 new numbers = 3 × 16 = 48 Total sum = 240 + 48 = 288 New average = 288/23 = 12.52

**Answer: 12.52**

</details>

## TYPE 3: Replacement Problems

**Q7.** The average of 5 numbers is 40. If one number (50) is replaced by 70, what is the new average?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

**Quick Method:** Increase in sum = 70 - 50 = 20 Increase in average = 20/5 = 4 New average = 40 + 4 = 44

**Standard Method:** Original sum = 5 × 40 = 200 New sum = 200 - 50 + 70 = 220 New average = 220/5 = 44

**Answer: 44**

</details>

**Q8.** Average of 15 numbers is 35. If each number is replaced by a number 5 more than itself, what is the new average?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

When each number increases by 5, average also increases by 5. New average = 35 + 5 = 40

**Rule:** If each element changes by 'k', average changes by 'k'

**Answer: 40**

</details>

**Q9.** Average weight of 10 persons increases by 2.5 kg when one person weighing 65 kg is replaced by a new person. Find the weight of the new person.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Total increase = 10 × 2.5 = 25 kg Weight of new person = 65 + 25 = 90 kg

**Answer: 90 kg**

</details>

## TYPE 4: Grouped Averages

**Q10.** Average of 25 numbers is 18, average of first 13 numbers is 14, and average of last 13 numbers is 22. Find the 13th number.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Sum of all 25 numbers = 25 × 18 = 450 Sum of first 13 = 13 × 14 = 182 Sum of last 13 = 13 × 22 = 286

13th number is counted twice in (182 + 286) 13th number = 182 + 286 - 450 = 18

**Answer: 18**

</details>

**Q11.** Average of 11 numbers is 50. Average of first 6 numbers is 49 and last 6 numbers is 52. Find the 6th number.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Sum of 11 numbers = 11 × 50 = 550 Sum of first 6 = 6 × 49 = 294 Sum of last 6 = 6 × 52 = 312

6th number = 294 + 312 - 550 = 56

**Answer: 56**

</details>

**Q12.** In a class of 50 students, average marks of 30 students is 60 and average of remaining 20 students is 70. Find overall average.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

**Weighted Average Method:** Average = (30×60 + 20×70)/(30+20) = (1800 + 1400)/50 = 3200/50 = 64

**Answer: 64**

</details>

## TYPE 6: Age-Related Average Problems

**Q17.** The average age of a family of 5 members is 24 years. If the age of the youngest member is 8 years, what was the average age of the family at the birth of the youngest member?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Present total age = 5 × 24 = 120 years 8 years ago (at birth of youngest):

- Youngest didn't exist (4 members)
- Each of other 4 was 8 years younger Total age then = 120 - (5×8) = 120 - 40 = 80 Average = 80/4 = 20 years

**Answer: 20 years**

</details>

**Q18.** Average age of 30 students is 15 years. Average age of 20 of them is 14 years. Find average age of remaining 10 students.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Total age of 30 students = 30 × 15 = 450 Total age of 20 students = 20 × 14 = 280 Total age of 10 students = 450 - 280 = 170 Average = 170/10 = 17 years

**Answer: 17 years**

</details>

**Q19.** Average age of husband and wife was 27 years when they married 5 years ago. Now the average age of husband, wife, and child is 22 years. What is the child's age?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

5 years ago: Combined age of couple = 27 × 2 = 54 Now: Combined age of couple = 54 + (5×2) = 64

Now: Total age of 3 = 22 × 3 = 66 Child's age = 66 - 64 = 2 years

**Answer: 2 years**

</details>

## TYPE 7: Speed/Distance Average Problems

**Q20.** A man travels 100 km at 50 km/hr and returns at 40 km/hr. Find average speed for the whole journey.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

**Formula for equal distances:** Average speed = (2 × S₁ × S₂)/(S₁ + S₂) = (2 × 50 × 40)/(50 + 40) = 4000/90 = 44.44 km/hr

**NOT (50+40)/2 = 45** ← Common mistake!

**Answer: 44.44 km/hr**

</details>

**Q21.** A person covers 1/3 distance at 10 km/hr, 1/3 at 20 km/hr, and remaining at 30 km/hr. Find average speed.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let total distance = 3d (for easy calculation)

Time₁ = d/10 Time₂ = d/20  
Time₃ = d/30

Total time = d/10 + d/20 + d/30 = d(6+3+2)/60 = 11d/60

Average speed = Total distance/Total time = 3d/(11d/60) = 180/11 = 16.36 km/hr

**Answer: 16.36 km/hr**

</details>

**Q22.** A car travels for 3 hours at 40 km/hr and for 2 hours at 60 km/hr. Find average speed.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Total distance = 3×40 + 2×60 = 120 + 120 = 240 km Total time = 3 + 2 = 5 hours Average speed = 240/5 = 48 km/hr

**Answer: 48 km/hr**

</details>

## TYPE 10: Cricket/Sports Averages

**Q28.** A batsman has an average of 40 runs in 10 innings. How many runs should he score in the 11th innings to increase his average by 2?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Current total = 40 × 10 = 400 Required average = 42 Required total = 42 × 11 = 462 Runs needed = 462 - 400 = 62

**Answer: 62 runs**

</details>

**Q29.** A cricketer has an average of 50 runs in 20 innings. In next 5 innings, he scores average of 70. What is his new overall average?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Total in 20 innings = 50 × 20 = 1000 Total in 5 innings = 70 × 5 = 350 Overall total = 1350 New average = 1350/25 = 54

**Answer: 54**

</details>

**Q30.** A player's batting average increases by 5 when he scores 100 in his 11th innings. What was his average before the 11th innings?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let previous average = x Total runs in 10 innings = 10x After 11th innings: (10x + 100)/11 = x + 5

10x + 100 = 11x + 55 100 - 55 = x x = 45

**Previous average = 45 runs**

</details>

**Q31.** Average temperature from Monday to Wednesday is 30°C and from Tuesday to Thursday is 32°C. If Monday's temperature is 28°C, find Thursday's temperature.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

## TYPE 13: Tricky/Advanced Questions

**Q33.** Average of n numbers is 'a'. If first number is increased by 1, second by 2, third by 3, and so on, what is the new average?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Total increase = 1+2+3+...+n = n(n+1)/2 Increase in average = n(n+1)/(2n) = (n+1)/2

New average = a + (n+1)/2

**Answer: a + (n+1)/2**

</details>

**Q34.** Average of 50 numbers is 38. Later it was found that number 63 was misread as 36. What is the correct average?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Wrong sum = 50 × 38 = 1900 Difference = 63 - 36 = 27 Correct sum = 1900 + 27 = 1927 Correct average = 1927/50 = 38.54

**Answer: 38.54**

</details>

**Q35.** Average of 5 consecutive odd numbers is 27. Find the largest number.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Average of consecutive numbers = middle number Middle (3rd) number = 27

Numbers: 23, 25, 27, 29, 31 Largest = 31

**Answer: 31**

</details>

**Q36.** Average of 6 consecutive even numbers is 25. Find the difference between largest and smallest.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

For 6 consecutive even numbers, average falls between 3rd and 4th Average = (3rd + 4th)/2 = 25 3rd + 4th = 50

If 3rd = 24, 4th = 26 Numbers: 20, 22, 24, 26, 28, 30

Difference = 30 - 20 = 10

**Answer: 10**

</details>
