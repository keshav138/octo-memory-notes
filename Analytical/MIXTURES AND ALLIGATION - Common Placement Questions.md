## TYPE 1: Basic Alligation Method

**Q1.** In what ratio must tea at ₹62/kg be mixed with tea at ₹72/kg so that the mixture costs ₹64.50/kg?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Using Alligation Rule:

```
        Tea at ₹72         Tea at ₹62
              \              /
               \            /
                \          /
            Mean Price ₹64.50
                /          \
               /            \
              /              \
        (64.50-62)      (72-64.50)
           2.50             7.50
```

Ratio = 2.50 : 7.50 = 1:3

**Answer: 1:3**

</details>

**Q2.** A merchant has two types of rice, one at ₹15/kg and other at ₹20/kg. In what ratio should they be mixed to get a mixture worth ₹17/kg?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

```
        ₹20              ₹15
          \              /
           \            /
            \          /
           Mean ₹17
            /          \
           /            \
          /              \
       (17-15)        (20-17)
          2              3
```

Ratio = 2:3

**Answer: 2:3**

</details>

**Q3.** Two varieties of wheat at ₹9/kg and ₹14/kg are mixed in ratio 3:7. Find the price per kg of mixture.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Price of mixture = (3×9 + 7×14)/(3+7)
= (27 + 98)/10
= 125/10 = ₹12.50/kg

**Answer: ₹12.50/kg**

</details>

## TYPE 2: Milk and Water Mixture Problems

**Q4.** A vessel contains 60 liters of milk. 12 liters are removed and replaced with water. This process is repeated once more. Find the quantity of milk left.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

After 1st operation:
Milk left = 60 × (1 - 12/60) = 60 × 48/60 = 48 liters

After 2nd operation:
Milk left = 48 × (1 - 12/60) = 48 × 48/60 = 38.4 liters

**Formula: Final quantity = Initial × (1 - d/V)ⁿ**
where d = quantity removed, V = total volume, n = operations

= 60 × (48/60)² = 60 × 0.64 = 38.4 liters

**Answer: 38.4 liters**

</details>

**Q5.** A container has 40 liters of milk. 4 liters are removed and replaced with water. If this is repeated 3 times, how much milk remains?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Using formula: Final = Initial × (1 - d/V)ⁿ

= 40 × (1 - 4/40)³
= 40 × (36/40)³
= 40 × (0.9)³
= 40 × 0.729
= 29.16 liters

**Answer: 29.16 liters**

</details>

**Q6.** In a mixture of 60 liters, ratio of milk to water is 2:1. How much water should be added to make ratio 1:2?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Original: Milk = 40L, Water = 20L

Let x liters of water be added
New ratio: 40:(20+x) = 1:2

2(40) = 1(20+x)
80 = 20 + x
x = 60 liters

**Answer: 60 liters**

</details>

## TYPE 3: Replacement and Removal Problems

**Q7.** A jar contains 50 liters of pure milk. 5 liters is drawn out and replaced with water. The process is repeated 2 more times. What is the ratio of milk to water in final mixture?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

After 3 operations:
Milk left = 50 × (45/50)³ = 50 × (0.9)³ = 50 × 0.729 = 36.45 liters
Water = 50 - 36.45 = 13.55 liters

Ratio = 36.45:13.55 = 729:271

**Answer: 729:271 (approximately 27:10)**

</details>

**Q8.** A vessel has 80 liters mixture of milk and water in ratio 3:1. How many liters must be drawn off and replaced with water to make ratio 1:1?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Original: Milk = 60L, Water = 20L

Let x liters be removed and replaced
Milk removed = (3/4)x
Milk remaining = 60 - (3/4)x

Water = 20 - (1/4)x + x = 20 + (3/4)x

For ratio 1:1:
60 - (3/4)x = 20 + (3/4)x
40 = (3/2)x
x = 80/3 = 26.67 liters

**Answer: 26.67 liters**

</details>

## TYPE 4: Multiple Ingredient Mixing

**Q9.** Three vessels contain mixtures of milk and water in ratios 2:1, 3:2, and 5:3. If equal quantities from each are mixed, find the ratio of milk to water in new mixture.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Vessel 1: Milk = 2/3, Water = 1/3
Vessel 2: Milk = 3/5, Water = 2/5
Vessel 3: Milk = 5/8, Water = 3/8

Total milk = 2/3 + 3/5 + 5/8 = (80+72+75)/120 = 227/120
Total water = 1/3 + 2/5 + 3/8 = (40+48+45)/120 = 133/120

Ratio = 227:133

**Answer: 227:133**

</details>

**Q10.** Two vessels A and B contain milk and water in ratios 4:3 and 2:3. In what ratio should they be mixed to get a mixture with milk to water ratio 5:4?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

In vessel A: Milk fraction = 4/7
In vessel B: Milk fraction = 2/5
In final mixture: Milk fraction = 5/9

Using alligation:
```
        4/7              2/5
          \              /
           \            /
            \          /
           5/9
            /          \
           /            \
          /              \
    (5/9 - 2/5)      (4/7 - 5/9)
    (25-18)/45       (36-35)/63
      7/45             1/63
```

Ratio = (7/45):(1/63) = (7×63):(45×1) = 441:45 = 49:5

**Answer: 49:5**

</details>

## TYPE 5: Wine/Spirit Mixture Problems

**Q11.** A mixture of 150 liters of wine and water contains 20% water. How much water must be added to make water 25% in the new mixture?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Original water = 20% of 150 = 30 liters
Wine = 150 - 30 = 120 liters

Let x liters of water be added
New water = 30 + x
Total mixture = 150 + x

(30 + x)/(150 + x) = 25/100 = 1/4
4(30 + x) = 150 + x
120 + 4x = 150 + x
3x = 30
x = 10 liters

**Answer: 10 liters**

</details>

**Q12.** A 40-liter mixture of milk and water contains milk and water in ratio 3:1. How much mixture must be drawn off and replaced with pure milk to get milk and water in ratio 4:1?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Original: Milk = 30L, Water = 10L

Let x liters be removed
Milk removed = (3/4)x, Water removed = (1/4)x

After adding pure milk (x liters):
Milk = 30 - (3/4)x + x = 30 + (1/4)x
Water = 10 - (1/4)x

For ratio 4:1:
[30 + (1/4)x]/[10 - (1/4)x] = 4/1
30 + (1/4)x = 40 - x
(5/4)x = 10
x = 8 liters

**Answer: 8 liters**

</details>

```
        14%              -6%
          \              /
           \            /
            \          /
            8%
            /          \
           /            \
          /              \
       (8-(-6))        (14-8)
          14              6
```

Ratio = 14:6 = 7:3

Quantity at 14% profit = (7/10) × 50 = 35 kg

**Answer: 35 kg**

</details>

**Q23.** 8 liters are drawn from a cask full of wine and replaced with water. This is repeated 3 times. The ratio of wine to water is then 8:19. Find the capacity of cask.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let capacity = V liters

After 3 operations:
Wine left = V × (1 - 8/V)³

Given: Wine/Water = 8/19
Wine/(Total-Wine) = 8/19
Wine/V = 8/27

V × (1 - 8/V)³ = 8V/27
(1 - 8/V)³ = 8/27
1 - 8/V = 2/3
8/V = 1/3
V = 24 liters

**Answer: 24 liters**

</details>

**Q26.** A jar full of whisky contains 40% alcohol. A part of this is replaced with another containing 19% alcohol. Now the ratio becomes 26%. How much of original mixture was replaced?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let fraction replaced = x

(1-x)×40 + x×19 = 26
40 - 40x + 19x = 26
40 - 21x = 26
21x = 14
x = 14/21 = 2/3

**Answer: 2/3 of original mixture**

## TYPE 13: Tricky Application Problems

**Q29.** A dishonest milkman professes to sell milk at CP but dilutes it with 20% water. Find his profit percentage.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let CP of 1L milk = ₹100
He adds 20% water, so for every 80L milk, adds 20L water = 100L mixture

CP for him = ₹8,000 (for 80L)
SP = ₹10,000 (sells 100L at milk price)

Profit = 10,000 - 8,000 = ₹2,000
Profit% = (2000/8000) × 100 = 25%

**Answer: 25%**

</details>

## KEY FORMULAS SUMMARY

1. **Alligation Rule:**
   ```
   (Cheaper quantity)/(Dearer quantity) = (Mean - Cheaper)/(Dearer - Mean)
   ```

2. **Replacement Formula:**
   After n operations: Final quantity = Initial × (1 - d/V)ⁿ
   where d = removed quantity, V = total volume

3. **Mean Price:**
   Mean = (Q₁P₁ + Q₂P₂ + ...)/(Q₁ + Q₂ + ...)

4. **For percentage solutions:**
   Final % = (Total quantity of substance)/(Total volume) × 100

## Practice Strategy for Mixtures & Alligations:

1. Master the alligation diagram method - it's the fastest
2. Practice replacement formula for repeated operations
3. Always convert ratios to fractions when mixing multiple mixtures
4. For wine/water problems, track each component separately
5. Draw diagrams for complex multi-step problems
6. Remember: When equal quantities are mixed, mean is simple average
7. Time target: 90-120 seconds per question

**Pro Tips:**
- Alligation works for any weighted average problem (price, concentration, percentage)
- In replacement problems, use the formula instead of step-by-step for speed
- For 3+ mixtures, solve in pairs using alligation twice
- Always verify final answer by checking if proportions add up correctly
- Convert all percentages to the same base before mixing
