
### Core Concepts

- **Ratio**: Comparison of two quantities (a:b)
- **Proportion**: Equality of two ratios (a:b = c:d or a/b = c/d)
- In a:b = c:d, product of extremes = product of means (a×d = b×c)

### Important Formulas

- **Componendo**: If a/b = c/d, then (a+b)/b = (c+d)/d
- **Dividendo**: If a/b = c/d, then (a-b)/b = (c-d)/d
- **Componendo-Dividendo**: If a/b = c/d, then (a+b)/(a-b) = (c+d)/(c-d)
- **Fourth Proportional**: If a:b = c:d, then d is the fourth proportional
- **Third Proportional**: If a:b = b:c, then c is the third proportional (c = b²/a)
- **Mean Proportional**: If a:b = b:c, then b is the mean proportional (b = √(ac))

### Distribution Problems

- If a sum S is divided in ratio a:b:c, shares are:
    - First share = [a/(a+b+c)] × S
    - Second share = [b/(a+b+c)] × S
    - Third share = [c/(a+b+c)] × S

**Q13.** A, B, and C enter into a partnership with capitals in ratio 2:3:4. After 6 months, A withdraws half his capital. What is the ratio of their profits at year end?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Profit ratio = Capital × Time ratio

A: 2 × 6 + 1 × 6 = 12 + 6 = 18 B: 3 × 12 = 36 C: 4 × 12 = 48

**Profit ratio = 18:36:48 = 3:6:8**

</details>

**Q19.** A, B, and C can complete a work in 10, 12, and 15 days respectively. They started together but A left after 2 days and B left 3 days before completion. In how many days was the work completed?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let total days = x

A worked for 2 days, B worked for (x-3) days, C worked for x days

2(1/10) + (x-3)(1/12) + x(1/15) = 1 2/10 + (x-3)/12 + x/15 = 1

Multiply by 60: 12 + 5(x-3) + 4x = 60 12 + 5x - 15 + 4x = 60 9x = 63 x = 7

**Work completed in 7 days**

</details>

**Q20.** The speeds of A and B are in ratio 3:4. A takes 20 minutes more than B to reach a destination. Find the time taken by B.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Speed ratio = 3:4, so time ratio = 4:3 (inverse)

Let time taken by B = 3x, time by A = 4x 4
x - 3x = 20 
x = 20

**B takes 3×20 = 60 minutes = 1 hour**

</details>
