
## TYPE 1: Basic Profit & Loss Concepts

**Q1.** A shopkeeper buys an article for ₹500 and sells it for ₹650. Find the profit percentage.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Cost Price (CP) = ₹500
Selling Price (SP) = ₹650
Profit = SP - CP = 650 - 500 = ₹150

Profit% = (Profit/CP) × 100 = (150/500) × 100 = 30%

**Answer: 30% profit**

</details>

**Q2.** A trader sells an item at ₹840 after giving a 20% loss. What was the cost price?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

SP = ₹840
Loss% = 20%

SP = CP × (100 - Loss%)/100
840 = CP × 80/100
CP = 840 × 100/80 = ₹1,050

**Answer: ₹1,050**

</details>

**Q3.** If selling price of 10 articles equals cost price of 12 articles, find profit percentage.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let CP of 1 article = ₹1
CP of 12 articles = ₹12
SP of 10 articles = ₹12

SP of 1 article = ₹1.20
Profit on 1 article = 1.20 - 1 = ₹0.20

Profit% = (0.20/1) × 100 = 20%

**Answer: 20% profit**

</details>

## TYPE 2: Discount Problems

**Q4.** A shirt with marked price ₹1,200 is sold at a discount of 15%. Find the selling price.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Marked Price (MP) = ₹1,200
Discount% = 15%

SP = MP × (100 - Discount%)/100
SP = 1200 × 85/100 = ₹1,020

**Answer: ₹1,020**

</details>

**Q5.** A shopkeeper marks goods 40% above cost price and gives a 20% discount. Find profit percentage.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let CP = ₹100
MP = 100 + 40% of 100 = ₹140

SP = MP - 20% of MP = 140 - 28 = ₹112

Profit = 112 - 100 = ₹12
Profit% = (12/100) × 100 = 12%

**Answer: 12% profit**

</details>

**Q6.** A dealer offers successive discounts of 20% and 10%. What is the single equivalent discount?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let MP = ₹100

After 1st discount (20%): Price = 100 - 20 = ₹80
After 2nd discount (10% on 80): Price = 80 - 8 = ₹72

Total discount = 100 - 72 = ₹28
Equivalent discount% = 28%

**Alternative Formula:**
Single discount = a + b - (ab/100) = 20 + 10 - (200/100) = 28%

**Answer: 28%**

</details>

## TYPE 3: Cost Price and Selling Price Relations

**Q7.** By selling an article for ₹240, a man loses 20%. At what price should he sell to gain 20%?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

SP at 20% loss = ₹240
CP × 80/100 = 240
CP = 240 × 100/80 = ₹300

For 20% gain:
SP = CP × 120/100 = 300 × 1.20 = ₹360

**Answer: ₹360**

</details>

**Q8.** A man sells two fans for ₹2,400 each. On one he gains 20% and on the other he loses 20%. Find overall profit or loss percentage.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

For 1st fan (20% gain):
CP₁ = 2400 × 100/120 = ₹2,000

For 2nd fan (20% loss):
CP₂ = 2400 × 100/80 = ₹3,000

Total CP = 2000 + 3000 = ₹5,000
Total SP = 2400 + 2400 = ₹4,800

Loss = 5000 - 4800 = ₹200
Loss% = (200/5000) × 100 = 4%

**Answer: 4% loss**

</details>

**Q9.** If the cost price of 20 articles equals selling price of 25 articles, find loss percentage.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let CP of 1 article = ₹1
CP of 20 articles = ₹20
SP of 25 articles = ₹20

SP of 1 article = ₹0.80
Loss on 1 article = 1 - 0.80 = ₹0.20

Loss% = (0.20/1) × 100 = 20%

**Answer: 20% loss**

</details>

## TYPE 4: Markup and Multiple Transaction Problems

**Q10.** A shopkeeper marks goods 25% above cost price and allows a discount such that he gains 10%. Find the discount percentage.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let CP = ₹100
MP = ₹125

For 10% gain: SP = ₹110

Discount = MP - SP = 125 - 110 = ₹15
Discount% = (15/125) × 100 = 12%

**Answer: 12% discount**

</details>

**Q11.** A trader allows 15% discount on marked price and still makes 36% profit. If cost price is ₹1,000, find marked price.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

CP = ₹1,000
Profit% = 36%

SP = CP × 136/100 = 1000 × 1.36 = ₹1,360

Let MP = x
After 15% discount: x × 85/100 = 1360
x = 1360 × 100/85 = ₹1,600

**Answer: ₹1,600**

</details>

**Q12.** A person buys an article, marks it 60% above cost, and sells at 25% discount. If he still makes ₹350 profit, find the cost price.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let CP = x
MP = x + 60% of x = 1.6x

SP = MP - 25% of MP = 1.6x × 0.75 = 1.2x

Profit = SP - CP = 1.2x - x = 0.2x = ₹350
x = 350/0.2 = ₹1,750

**Answer: ₹1,750**

</details>

## TYPE 5: Dishonest Dealer Problems

**Q13.** A dishonest dealer professes to sell at cost price but uses a weight of 900g instead of 1kg. Find his profit percentage.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

He pays for 900g but sells as 1000g

Profit = 1000 - 900 = 100g on 900g investment

Profit% = (100/900) × 100 = 11.11%

**Answer: 11.11% or 11 1/9 %**

</details>

**Q14.** A shopkeeper cheats by 10% while buying and 10% while selling. What is his overall profit percentage?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let actual quantity = 100g, price = ₹1 per g

Buying: Pays for 100g but gets 110g (Cost = ₹100)
Selling: Gives 90g but charges for 100g (Revenue = ₹100)

Actual cost for 90g = 90 × (100/110) = ₹81.82
Revenue = ₹100

Profit = 100 - 81.82 = ₹18.18
Profit% = (18.18/81.82) × 100 ≈ 22.22%

**Formula:** (200x + x²)/100 where x = 10
= (200×10 + 100)/100 = 21% (approximately)

**Answer: ~21% profit**

</details>

## TYPE 6: Partnership and Profit Distribution

**Q15.** A and B invest ₹30,000 and ₹40,000. They agree A will get 10% of profit for managing. If total profit is ₹14,000, what does each receive?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

A's management fee = 10% of 14,000 = ₹1,400
Remaining profit = 14,000 - 1,400 = ₹12,600

Capital ratio = 30,000 : 40,000 = 3:4

A's share from remaining = (3/7) × 12,600 = ₹5,400
B's share = (4/7) × 12,600 = ₹7,200

Total A receives = 1,400 + 5,400 = ₹6,800
B receives = ₹7,200

**Answer: A = ₹6,800, B = ₹7,200**

</details>

## TYPE 7: Overall Profit/Loss with Multiple Items

**Q16.** A merchant has 1,000 kg of sugar, part of which he sells at 8% profit and rest at 18% profit. He gains 14% on the whole. How much did he sell at 18% profit?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let quantity sold at 18% profit = x kg
Quantity sold at 8% profit = (1000 - x) kg

Using allegation or equation:
8x + 18(1000-x) = 14(1000)
8x + 18000 - 18x = 14000
-10x = -4000
x = 400 kg at 8%

Quantity at 18% = 1000 - 400 = 600 kg

**Answer: 600 kg**

</details>

**Q17.** A trader mixes two varieties of rice costing ₹15/kg and ₹20/kg in ratio 3:2. At what price should he sell to gain 20%?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

CP of mixture per kg = (3×15 + 2×20)/(3+2) = (45+40)/5 = ₹17/kg

For 20% gain:
SP = 17 × 120/100 = ₹20.40/kg

**Answer: ₹20.40/kg**

</details>

## TYPE 8: Conditional Profit/Loss Problems

**Q18.** If an article is sold for ₹240, there is 20% loss. To gain 20%, it should be sold for how much?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

At 20% loss:
CP × 80/100 = 240
CP = ₹300

For 20% gain:
SP = 300 × 120/100 = ₹360

**Answer: ₹360**

</details>

**Q19.** A man wants 20% profit. He estimates expenses will be 12.5% of selling price. What percent should he mark above cost price?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let CP = ₹100
Desired SP = ₹120 (for 20% profit)

Expenses = 12.5% of SP = 12.5% of 120 = ₹15

MP must cover: CP + Desired profit + Expenses
MP = 100 + 20 + 15 = ₹135

Markup% = (35/100) × 100 = 35%

**Answer: 35% above CP**

</details>

## TYPE 9: Faulty Weight and Measure Problems

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

For 25% gain, CP of 4 units = SP of 5 units

He sells 5 units at price of 5 units (milk price)
But cost is only 4 units of milk

So 5 units mixture contains 4 units milk and 1 unit water

**Ratio of milk to water = 4:1**

</details>

**Q21.** A grocer uses a faulty balance which weighs 10% less. He sells at CP. What is his actual profit%?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Customer pays for 1000g but gets only 900g

Profit = 100g on 900g cost

Profit% = (100/900) × 100 = 11.11%

**Answer: 11.11%**

</details>

## TYPE 10: Profit in Different Scenarios

**Q22.** A sells goods to B at 10% profit. B sells to C at 20% profit. If C pays ₹1,320, what did A pay?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let A's CP = x

A sells to B at 10% profit: B's CP = 1.1x
B sells to C at 20% profit: C's CP = 1.1x × 1.2 = 1.32x

1.32x = 1320
x = ₹1,000

**Answer: ₹1,000**

</details>

**Q23.** A reduction of 20% in price enables a person to buy 4 more articles for ₹800. Find the reduced price per article.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let original price = x per article
Original quantity = 800/x

New price = 0.8x
New quantity = 800/(0.8x) = 1000/x

Difference = 1000/x - 800/x = 200/x = 4
x = 50

Reduced price = 0.8 × 50 = ₹40

**Answer: ₹40**

</details>

## TYPE 11: Complex Mixed Problems

**Q24.** A trader marks price 50% above CP. He gives successive discounts of 20% and 10%. If he still makes ₹540 profit, find CP.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let CP = x
MP = 1.5x

After 20% discount: 1.5x × 0.8 = 1.2x
After 10% discount: 1.2x × 0.9 = 1.08x

SP = 1.08x
Profit = 1.08x - x = 0.08x = 540
x = 540/0.08 = ₹6,750

**Answer: ₹6,750**

</details>

**Q25.** By selling 33 meters of cloth, a trader gains the selling price of 11 meters. Find profit percentage.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let SP of 1 meter = ₹1
SP of 33 meters = ₹33
Profit = SP of 11 meters = ₹11

CP of 33 meters = SP - Profit = 33 - 11 = ₹22

Profit% = (11/22) × 100 = 50%

**Answer: 50%**

</details>

**Q26.** A man purchases 8 pens for ₹9 and sells 9 pens for ₹8. Find his loss percentage.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

CP of 8 pens = ₹9 → CP of 1 pen = ₹9/8
SP of 9 pens = ₹8 → SP of 1 pen = ₹8/9

Loss per pen = 9/8 - 8/9 = (81-64)/72 = 17/72

Loss% = (17/72)/(9/8) × 100 = (17/72) × (8/9) × 100
= (17 × 8)/(72 × 9) × 100 = 136/648 × 100 ≈ 20.99%

**Answer: ~21% loss**

</details>

## TYPE 12: Advanced Application Problems

**Q27.** A shopkeeper sells an article at 15% loss. If he had sold it for ₹30 more, he would have gained 9%. Find the cost price.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let CP = x

At 15% loss: SP₁ = 0.85x
At 9% gain: SP₂ = 1.09x

Difference: 1.09x - 0.85x = 30
0.24x = 30
x = ₹125

**Answer: ₹125**

</details>

**Q28.** A person sells 36 apples per rupee and loses 4%. How many apples per rupee should he sell to gain 44%?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

SP of 36 apples = ₹1
At 4% loss: CP of 36 apples = 1/0.96 = ₹1.042

For 44% gain: SP should be = 1.042 × 1.44 = ₹1.50

Number of apples per rupee = 36/1.50 = 24 apples

**Answer: 24 apples per rupee**

</details>

**Q29.** A trader gives 3 successive discounts of 10%, 20%, and 30%. What is the net selling price if marked price is ₹1,000?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

MP = ₹1,000

After 10% discount: 1000 × 0.9 = ₹900
After 20% discount: 900 × 0.8 = ₹720
After 30% discount: 720 × 0.7 = ₹504

**Answer: ₹504**

</details>

**Q30.** If selling price is doubled, profit triples. Find the profit percentage.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let CP = 100, original SP = x
Original profit = x - 100

When SP doubles: New SP = 2x
New profit = 2x - 100 = 3(x - 100)
2x - 100 = 3x - 300
x = 200

Original profit = 200 - 100 = 100
Profit% = (100/100) × 100 = 100%

**Answer: 100%**

</details>

## KEY FORMULAS SUMMARY

1. **Profit% = (Profit/CP) × 100**
2. **Loss% = (Loss/CP) × 100**
3. **SP = CP × (100 + Profit%)/100**
4. **SP = CP × (100 - Loss%)/100**
5. **Discount = MP - SP**
6. **SP = MP × (100 - Discount%)/100**
7. **Successive discounts: Single discount = a + b - (ab/100)**
8. **When SP is same with x% profit and x% loss: Always results in loss = (x/10)²%**

## Practice Strategy for Profit & Loss:

1. Master the basic formulas and their variations
2. Practice successive discount calculations
3. Understand dishonest dealer problems thoroughly
4. Learn to set up equations for complex scenarios
5. Remember: When equal SP with equal % gain and loss → always results in loss
6. Time target: 60-90 seconds per question

**Pro Tips:**
- Always identify what is given: CP, SP, MP, Discount, Profit/Loss
- Draw diagrams for multiple transaction problems
- Use approximations for quick mental calculations
- Watch for "professes to sell at CP" - usually means cheating involved
