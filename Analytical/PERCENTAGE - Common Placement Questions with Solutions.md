## TYPE 1: Basic Percentage Calculations

**Q1.** What is 35% of 840?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

35% of 840 = (35/100) × 840 = 294

**Quick Method:** 10% of 840 = 84 30% = 84 × 3 = 252 5% = 84/2 = 42 35% = 252 + 42 = 294

</details>

**Q2.** 65% of a number is 520. What is the number?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let the number be x 65% of x = 520 (65/100) × x = 520 x = (520 × 100)/65 = 800

</details>

## TYPE 2: Percentage Increase/Decrease

**Q3.** The price of a commodity increases from ₹500 to ₹650. What is the percentage increase?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Percentage increase = [(650 - 500)/500] × 100 = (150/500) × 100 = 30%

</details>

**Q4.** A number is increased by 20% and then decreased by 20%. What is the net change?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let original number = 100 After 20% increase = 120 After 20% decrease on 120 = 120 - 24 = 96

Net change = 96 - 100 = -4 **Net decrease = 4%**

**Formula:** Net change = -[(20 × 20)/100] = -4% `(Always a decrease when same percentage is increased then decreased)`

</details>

**Q5.** If A's salary is 25% more than B's salary, then B's salary is how much percent less than A's?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Formula: [x/(100+x)] × 100 = [25/125] × 100 = 20%

**Verification:** Let B's salary = 100 A's salary = 125 Difference = 25 Percentage less = (25/125) × 100 = 20%

</details>

## TYPE 3: Successive Percentage Changes

**Q6.** The population of a city increases by 10% in the first year, 15% in the second year, and 20% in the third year. What is the total percentage increase after 3 years?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let original population = 100 After Year 1 = 100 × 1.10 = 110 After Year 2 = 110 × 1.15 = 126.5 After Year 3 = 126.5 × 1.20 = 151.8

Total increase = 151.8 - 100 = 51.8%


**Quick Formula (2 changes):** a + b + (ab/100) For 3 changes, calculate two first, then apply with third.

</details>

**Q7.** A shopkeeper marks his goods 40% above cost price and gives a 20% discount. What is his profit percentage?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let CP = 100 MP = 140 SP = 140 × (80/100) = 112

Profit % = 12%

**Formula Method:** Net % = 40 - 20 - (40×20)/100 = 20 - 8 = 12%

</details>

**Q8.** The price of sugar decreases by 20%. By what percentage can consumption be increased so that expenditure remains the same?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Formula: [Decrease/(100 - Decrease)] × 100 = [20/(100-20)] × 100 = 20/80 × 100 = 25%

**Verification:** Original: Price = 100, Consumption = 100, Expenditure = 10,000 New: Price = 80, Consumption = 125, Expenditure = 10,000

</details>

## TYPE 4: Exam/Student Performance Questions

**Q9.** A student scores 40% marks and fails by 20 marks. Another student scores 55% and gets 10 marks more than the passing marks. Find the maximum marks and passing marks.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let maximum marks = M First student: 0.40M = Passing marks - 20 Second student: 0.55M = Passing marks + 10

From equations: 0.55M - 0.40M = 30 0.15M = 30 M = 200

Passing marks = 0.40 × 200 + 20 = 80 + 20 = 100

**Maximum marks = 200, Passing marks = 100**

</details>

**Q10.** In an exam, 70% students passed in English, 65% passed in Math, and 27% failed in both. What percentage passed in both subjects?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Failed in both = 27% Passed in at least one = 100 - 27 = 73%

Using formula: n(E ∪ M) = n(E) + n(M) - n(E ∩ M) 73 = 70 + 65 - n(E ∩ M) n(E ∩ M) = 135 - 73 = 62%

**62% passed in both subjects**

</details>

## TYPE 5: Income, Expenditure, Savings

**Q11.** A person's income increases by 20% and his expenditure increases by 10%. If his savings increase by 50%, what is the ratio of his income to expenditure?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let original income = I, expenditure = E Savings = I - E

New income = 1.2I New expenditure = 1.1E New savings = 1.5(I - E)

1.2I - 1.1E = 1.5I - 1.5E 1.5E - 1.1E = 1.5I - 1.2I 0.4E = 0.3I I/E = 4/3

**Ratio = 4:3**

</details>

**Q12.** Ram spends 70% of his income. His income increases by 20% and his expenditure increases by 10%. What is the percentage increase in his savings?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let income = 100 Expenditure = 70, Savings = 30

New income = 120 New expenditure = 70 × 1.1 = 77 New savings = 120 - 77 = 43

Increase in savings = 43 - 30 = 13 Percentage increase = (13/30) × 100 = 43.33%

</details>

## TYPE 6: Election/Voting Questions

**Q13.** In an election between two candidates, one got 55% of total valid votes. 20% of the votes were invalid. If the total votes were 15,000, how many valid votes did the other candidate get?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Valid votes = 80% of 15,000 = 12,000 First candidate = 55% of 12,000 = 6,600 Second candidate = 45% of 12,000 = 5,400

**Answer: 5,400 votes**

</details>

**Q14.** In an election, candidate A got 40% votes and lost by 2,400 votes. Find the total number of votes polled.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

A got 40%, so B got 60% Difference = 60% - 40% = 20%

20% of total votes = 2,400 Total votes = (2,400 × 100)/20 = 12,000

</details>

## TYPE 7: Population Growth/Depreciation

**Q15.** The population of a town is 125,000. If it increases at 4% per annum, what will be the population after 3 years?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Population after n years = P(1 + R/100)^n = 125,000 × (1.04)³ = 125,000 × 1.124864 = 140,608

**Answer: 140,608**

</details>

**Q16.** The value of a machine depreciates by 10% every year. If its present value is ₹50,000, what was its value 2 years ago?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Present value = Original value × (0.9)² <br/>
50,000 = Original value × 0.81<br/>
Original value = 50,000/0.81 = ₹61,728.40

</details>

## TYPE 8: Comparison-Based Questions

**Q17.** A is 50% more efficient than B. If B takes 30 days to complete a work, how many days will A take?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

If B's efficiency = 100, A's efficiency = 150

Time taken is inversely proportional to efficiency A's time = (100/150) × 30 = 20 days

</details>

**Q18.** The price of wheat falls by 16%. By how much percent can a person increase consumption so that expenditure increases only by 5%?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let original price = 100, consumption = 100 Expenditure = 10,000

New price = 84 New expenditure should be = 10,500 New consumption = 10,500/84 = 125

Increase in consumption = 25%

**Formula approach:** (100 + x)(100 - 16) = 105 × 100 84(100 + x) = 10,500 100 + x = 125 x = 25%

</details>

## TYPE 9: Mixture of Percentages with Other Topics

**Q19.** A scored 30% marks and failed by 15 marks. B scored 40% marks and obtained 35 marks more than the passing marks. What are the passing marks?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let maximum marks = M For A: 0.30M + 15 = Passing marks For B: 0.40M - 35 = Passing marks

0.30M + 15 = 0.40M - 35 50 = 0.10M M = 500

Passing marks = 0.30 × 500 + 15 = 165

</details>

**Q20.** In a school, 60% students are boys. If the number of girls is 400, find the total number of students.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Girls = 40% of total 40% of total = 400 Total = (400 × 100)/40 = 1,000 students

</details>

## TYPE 10: Tricky/Advanced Questions

**Q21.** A number is mistakenly divided by 5 instead of being multiplied by 5. What is the percentage error?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let the number be 10 Correct result = 10 × 5 = 50 Wrong result = 10/5 = 2

Error = 50 - 2 = 48 Percentage error = (48/50) × 100 = 96%

</details>

**Q22.** The price of a product is increased by 30%, then decreased by 30%, then increased by 30%. What is the final price if the original price was ₹100?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

After 1st increase: 100 × 1.30 = 130 After decrease: 130 × 0.70 = 91 After 2nd increase: 91 × 1.30 = 118.30

**Final price = ₹118.30**

Net change = 18.3% increase

</details>

**Q23.** Fresh fruit contains 68% water and dry fruit contains 20% water. How much dry fruit can be obtained from 100 kg of fresh fruit?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Fresh fruit: Water = 68 kg, Solid = 32 kg Dry fruit: Water = 20%, Solid = 80%

Solid content remains same Let dry fruit = x kg <br/>
80% of x = 32 <br/>
x = 32/0.8 = 40 kg

**Answer: 40 kg**

</details>

**Q24.** The price of oil is increased by 25%. By how much percent must a family reduce its consumption to keep the expenditure the same?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Formula: [Increase/(100 + Increase)] × 100 = [25/125] × 100 = 20%

**Must reduce consumption by 20%**

</details>

**Q25.** In a test, minimum passing percentage is 45%. A student gets 180 marks and fails by 45 marks. What is the maximum marks?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Passing marks = 180 + 45 = 225 45% of maximum = 225 Maximum = (225 × 100)/45 = 500 marks

</details>
