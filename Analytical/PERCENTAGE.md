1. PERCENTAGE

### Core Concepts

- Percentage means "per hundred" (%)
- To convert fraction to percentage: (Fraction × 100)%
- To convert percentage to fraction: Percentage/100

### Important Formulas

- **Percentage Increase**: [(New Value - Original Value)/Original Value] × 100
- **Percentage Decrease**: [(Original Value - New Value)/Original Value] × 100
- **Percentage Point Difference**: Direct subtraction (e.g., 60% - 40% = 20 percentage points)
- If A is x% more than B, then B is less than A by: [x/(100+x)] × 100%
- If A is x% less than B, then B is more than A by: [x/(100-x)] × 100%

### Key Shortcuts

- **Successive Percentage Changes**: If two successive changes of a% and b% occur, net change = [a + b + (ab/100)]%
- **Fraction to Percentage Quick Conversions**:
    - 1/2 = 50%, 1/3 = 33.33%, 1/4 = 25%, 1/5 = 20%
    - 1/6 = 16.66%, 1/7 = 14.28%, 1/8 = 12.5%, 1/9 = 11.11%, 1/10 = 10%

Situational Formulae

**Q4.** A number is increased by 20% and then decreased by 20%. What is the net change?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let original number = 100 After 20% increase = 120 After 20% decrease on 120 = 120 - 24 = 96

Net change = 96 - 100 = -4 **Net decrease = 4%**

**Formula:** Net change = -[(20 × 20)/100] = -4% `(Always a decrease when same percentage is increased then decreased)`

</details>

**Q8.** The price of sugar decreases by 20%. By what percentage can consumption be increased so that expenditure remains the same?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Formula: [Decrease/(100 - Decrease)] × 100 = [20/(100-20)] × 100 = 20/80 × 100 = 25%

**Verification:** Original: Price = 100, Consumption = 100, Expenditure = 10,000 New: Price = 80, Consumption = 125, Expenditure = 10,000

</details>

**Q10.** In an exam, 70% students passed in English, 65% passed in Math, and 27% failed in both. What percentage passed in both subjects?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Failed in both = 27% Passed in at least one = 100 - 27 = 73%

Using formula: n(E ∪ M) = n(E) + n(M) - n(E ∩ M) 73 = 70 + 65 - n(E ∩ M) n(E ∩ M) = 135 - 73 = 62%

**62% passed in both subjects**

</details>

**Q11.** A person's income increases by 20% and his expenditure increases by 10%. If his savings increase by 50%, what is the ratio of his income to expenditure?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let original income = I, expenditure = E Savings = I - E

New income = 1.2I New expenditure = 1.1E New savings = 1.5(I - E)

1.2I - 1.1E = 1.5I - 1.5E 1.5E - 1.1E = 1.5I - 1.2I 0.4E = 0.3I I/E = 4/3

**Ratio = 4:3**

</details>


**Q15.** The population of a town is 125,000. If it increases at 4% per annum, what will be the population after 3 years?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Population after n years = P(1 + R/100)^n = 125,000 × (1.04)³ = 125,000 × 1.124864 = 140,608

**Answer: 140,608**

</details>

**Q23.** Fresh fruit contains 68% water and dry fruit contains 20% water. How much dry fruit can be obtained from 100 kg of fresh fruit?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Fresh fruit: Water = 68 kg, Solid = 32 kg Dry fruit: Water = 20%, Solid = 80%

Solid content remains same Let dry fruit = x kg <br/>
80% of x = 32 <br/>
x = 32/0.8 = 40 kg

**Answer: 40 kg**

</details>
