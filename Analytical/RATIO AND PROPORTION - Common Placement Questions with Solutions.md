## TYPE 1: Basic Ratio Concepts

**Q1.** Divide ₹1360 among A, B, and C in the ratio 2:3:3.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Total parts = 2 + 3 + 3 = 8

A's share = (2/8) × 1360 = ₹340 B's share = (3/8) × 1360 = ₹510 C's share = (3/8) × 1360 = ₹510

**Answer: ₹340, ₹510, ₹510**

</details>

**Q2.** The ratio of boys to girls in a class is 5:3. If there are 40 students in total, how many girls are there?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Total parts = 5 + 3 = 8 Girls = (3/8) × 40 = 15

**Answer: 15 girls**

</details>

**Q3.** If A:B = 2:3 and B:C = 4:5, find A:B:C.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Make B equal in both ratios: A:B = 2:3 = 8:12 (multiply by 4) B:C = 4:5 = 12:15 (multiply by 3)

**A:B:C = 8:12:15**

</details>

## TYPE 2: Finding Individual Quantities from Ratios

**Q4.** The ratio of ages of A and B is 5:7. After 4 years, the ratio becomes 3:4. Find their present ages.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let present ages be 5x and 7x

After 4 years: (5x + 4):(7x + 4) = 3:4 4(5x + 4) = 3(7x + 4) 20x + 16 = 21x + 12 x = 4

Present ages: A = 5×4 = 20 years, B = 7×4 = 28 years

</details>

**Q5.** Two numbers are in the ratio 3:4. If 8 is added to each, the ratio becomes 5:6. Find the numbers.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let numbers be 3x and 4x

(3x + 8)/(4x + 8) = 5/6 6(3x + 8) = 5(4x + 8) 18x + 48 = 20x + 40 2x = 8 x = 4

**Numbers are 12 and 16**

</details>

**Q6.** The salaries of A, B, and C are in ratio 2:3:5. If the total salary is ₹50,000, and B's salary increases by ₹5,000, what will be the new ratio?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

A's salary = (2/10) × 50,000 = ₹10,000 B's salary = (3/10) × 50,000 = ₹15,000 C's salary = (5/10) × 50,000 = ₹25,000

New B's salary = 15,000 + 5,000 = ₹20,000

New ratio = 10,000 : 20,000 : 25,000 = 2:4:5

</details>

## TYPE 3: Proportion Problems

**Q7.** If 15 men can complete a work in 20 days, how many men are required to complete the same work in 12 days?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Men and days are inversely proportional. M₁ × D₁ = M₂ × D₂ 15 × 20 = M₂ × 12 M₂ = 300/12 = 25 men

**Answer: 25 men**

</details>

**Q8.** Find the fourth proportional to 3, 7, and 9.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

If a, b, c, d are in proportion: a:b = c:d 3:7 = 9:d 3d = 7 × 9 d = 63/3 = 21

**Answer: 21**

</details>

**Q9.** Find the third proportional to 4 and 12.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Third proportional means: a:b = b:c 4:12 = 12:c 4c = 144 c = 36

**Answer: 36**

</details>

**Q10.** Find the mean proportional between 9 and 16.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Mean proportional = √(a × b) = √(9 × 16) = √144 = 12

**Answer: 12**

</details>

## TYPE 4: Compound Ratio Questions

**Q11.** If A:B = 3:4, B:C = 8:9, and C:D = 15:16, find A:D.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

A:B = 3:4 B:C = 8:9 = (multiply by 1) = 8:9 C:D = 15:16

Make B and C equal: A:B = 3:4 = 90:120 (multiply by 30) B:C = 8:9 = 120:135 (multiply by 15) C:D = 15:16 = 135:144 (multiply by 9)

**A:D = 90:144 = 5:8**

</details>

**Q12.** The incomes of A and B are in ratio 5:3 and their expenditures are in ratio 9:5. If each saves ₹2,000, find their incomes.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let incomes be 5x and 3x Let expenditures be 9y and 5y

Savings: 5x - 9y = 2000 ... (1) 3x - 5y = 2000 ... (2)

From (1): 5x = 9y + 2000 From (2): 3x = 5y + 2000

Multiply (2) by 5 and (1) by 3: 15x - 25y = 10,000 15x - 27y = 6,000

Subtracting: 2y = 4,000, y = 2,000 Substituting: 3x - 10,000 = 2,000, x = 4,000

**A's income = ₹20,000, B's income = ₹12,000**

</details>

## TYPE 5: Mixture/Partnership Related Ratios

**Q13.** A, B, and C enter into a partnership with capitals in ratio 2:3:4. After 6 months, A withdraws half his capital. What is the ratio of their profits at year end?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Profit ratio = Capital × Time ratio

A: 2 × 6 + 1 × 6 = 12 + 6 = 18 
B: 3 × 12 = 36
C: 4 × 12 = 48

**Profit ratio = 18:36:48 = 3:6:8**

</details>

**Q14.** Two partners A and B invest ₹30,000 and ₹40,000 respectively. A receives 10% of profit for managing the business, and the rest is shared in ratio of capital. If A receives ₹1,900 out of total profit ₹5,000, is this correct?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

A's management fee = 10% of 5,000 = ₹500 Remaining profit = 5,000 - 500 = ₹4,500

Capital ratio = 30,000:40,000 = 3:4 A's share from remaining = (3/7) × 4,500 = ₹1,928.57

Total A receives = 500 + 1,928.57 = ₹2,428.57

**No, A should receive ₹2,428.57, not ₹1,900**

</details>

## TYPE 6: Age-Related Ratio Problems

**Q15.** The present ages of A and B are in ratio 3:5. Five years ago, the ratio was 1:2. Find their present ages.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let present ages be 3x and 5x

Five years ago: (3x - 5):(5x - 5) = 1:2 2(3x - 5) = 1(5x - 5) 6x - 10 = 5x - 5 x = 5

**Present ages: A = 15 years, B = 25 years**

</details>

**Q16.** The ratio of father's age to son's age is 7:3. After 5 years, the ratio will be 2:1. Find their present ages.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let present ages be 7x and 3x

After 5 years: (7x + 5):(3x + 5) = 2:1 1(7x + 5) = 2(3x + 5) 7x + 5 = 6x + 10 x = 5

**Father = 35 years, Son = 15 years**

</details>

**Q17.** Ten years ago, the ratio of ages of A and B was 3:5. Ten years hence, it will be 5:7. Find their present ages.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let present ages be a and b

Ten years ago: (a - 10):(b - 10) = 3:5 5(a - 10) = 3(b - 10) 5a - 50 = 3b - 30 5a - 3b = 20 ... (1)

Ten years hence: (a + 10):(b + 10) = 5:7 7(a + 10) = 5(b + 10) 7a + 70 = 5b + 50 7a - 5b = -20 ... (2)

From (1): 5a = 3b + 20 Multiply by 7: 35a = 21b + 140 Multiply (2) by 5: 35a = 25b - 100

21b + 140 = 25b - 100 4b = 240 b = 60

From (1): 5a = 180 + 20 = 200, a = 40

**A = 40 years, B = 60 years**

</details>

## TYPE 7: Work/Efficiency Based Ratios

**Q18.** A and B can do a work in 12 days and 15 days respectively. They work together for 4 days and then A leaves. In how many more days will B finish the remaining work?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

A's 1 day work = 1/12 B's 1 day work = 1/15

Together in 4 days = 4(1/12 + 1/15) = 4(9/60) = 36/60 = 3/5

Remaining work = 1 - 3/5 = 2/5

Time for B to finish 2/5 work = (2/5)/(1/15) = (2/5) × 15 = 6 days

</details>

**Q19.** A, B, and C can complete a work in 10, 12, and 15 days respectively. They started together but A left after 2 days and B left 3 days before completion. In how many days was the work completed?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let total days = x

A worked for 2 days, B worked for (x-3) days, C worked for x days

2(1/10) + (x-3)(1/12) + x(1/15) = 1 2/10 + (x-3)/12 + x/15 = 1

Multiply by 60: 12 + 5(x-3) + 4x = 60 12 + 5x - 15 + 4x = 60 9x = 63 x = 7

**Work completed in 7 days**

</details>

## TYPE 8: Speed/Distance Ratio Problems

**Q20.** The speeds of A and B are in ratio 3:4. A takes 20 minutes more than B to reach a destination. Find the time taken by B.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Speed ratio = 3:4, so time ratio = 4:3 (inverse)

Let time taken by B = 3x, time by A = 4x 4
x - 3x = 20 
x = 20

**B takes 3×20 = 60 minutes = 1 hour**

</details>

**Q21.** Two trains of lengths 100m and 150m are running at speeds in ratio 2:3. If they cross each other in 10 seconds (opposite directions), find their speeds.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let speeds be 2x and 3x Relative speed = 2x + 3x = 5x Total length = 100 + 150 = 250m

Time = Distance/Speed 10 = 250/5x 5x = 25 x = 5

**Speeds are 10 m/s and 15 m/s** (or 36 km/hr and 54 km/hr)

</details>

## TYPE 9: Componendo-Dividendo Applications

**Q22.** If (a+b)/(a-b) = 4, find a:b.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

(a+b)/(a-b) = 4 a + b = 4a - 4b 5b = 3a a/b = 5/3

**a:b = 5:3**

</details>

**Q23.** If (x+y):(x-y) = 5:3, find x:y.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

(x+y)/(x-y) = 5/3 3(x+y) = 5(x-y) 3x + 3y = 5x - 5y 8y = 2x x/y = 4/1

**x:y = 4:1**

</details>

## TYPE 10: Complex Ratio Problems

**Q24.** A sum of money is divided among A, B, C, and D in ratio 2:3:5:8. If C gets ₹1,000 more than B, find the total sum.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Ratio = 2:3:5:8 C's share - B's share = 5 parts - 3 parts = 2 parts = ₹1,000 1 part = ₹500

Total parts = 2+3+5+8 = 18 Total sum = 18 × 500 = ₹9,000

</details>

**Q25.** A bag contains coins of ₹1, ₹2, and ₹5 in ratio 4:8:5. If total value is ₹90, find number of ₹2 coins.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let number of coins be 4x, 8x, and 5x

Total value = 1(4x) + 2(8x) + 5(5x) = 90 4x + 16x + 25x = 90 45x = 90 x = 2

**Number of ₹2 coins = 8×2 = 16**

</details>

**Q26.** In a mixture of 60 liters, ratio of milk to water is 2:1. How much water should be added to make ratio 1:2?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Current: Milk = 40L, Water = 20L

Let x liters of water be added New ratio: 40:(20+x) = 1:2 2(40) = 1(20+x) 80 = 20 + x x = 60

**60 liters of water should be added**

</details>

## TYPE 11: Tricky/Advanced Questions

**Q27.** A, B, and C have amounts in ratio 3:4:5. If A gives ₹500 to C, the new ratio becomes 5:8:11. Find initial amounts.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let amounts be 3x, 4x, 5x

After transaction: (3x-500):4x:(5x+500) = 5:8:11

Using first and second: (3x-500)/4x = 5/8 8(3x-500) = 5(4x) 24x - 4000 = 20x 4x = 4000 x = 1000

**Initial amounts: A=₹3,000, B=₹4,000, C=₹5,000**

</details>

**Q29.** A, B, C invest in ratio 3:4:5. After 1 year, each increases investment by 50%. What's the profit ratio for second year?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Initial: 3x, 4x, 5x After increase: 4.5x, 6x, 7.5x

Second year profit ratio = 4.5:6:7.5 = 3:4:5

**Ratio remains 3:4:5**

</details>

**Q30.** The ratio of number of boys and girls is 3:2. If 20% of boys and 30% of girls are scholarship holders, what percentage of total students are scholarship holders?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let boys = 3x = 300 (take x=100 for easy calculation) Girls = 2x = 200 Total = 500

Scholarship boys = 20% of 300 = 60 Scholarship girls = 30% of 200 = 60 Total scholarship = 120

Percentage = (120/500) × 100 = 24%
