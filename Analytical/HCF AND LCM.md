### Definitions

- **HCF (Highest Common Factor)**: Largest number that divides all given numbers
- **LCM (Least Common Multiple)**: Smallest number divisible by all given numbers

### Important Formulas

- **For two numbers**: HCF × LCM = Product of the two numbers
- **HCF ≤ Each number ≤ LCM**
- **HCF divides LCM**

### Methods to Find HCF

1. **Prime Factorization**: Product of lowest powers of common prime factors
2. **Division Method (Euclid's Algorithm)**: Divide larger by smaller, then smaller by remainder, continue until remainder is 0

### Methods to Find LCM

1. **Prime Factorization**: Product of highest powers of all prime factors
2. **Division Method**: Divide by common factors, multiply all divisors and remaining numbers

### Key Concepts

- HCF of fractions = (HCF of numerators)/(LCM of denominators)
- LCM of fractions = (LCM of numerators)/(HCF of denominators)
- **Co-prime numbers**: HCF = 1, LCM = Product of numbers

### Practice Problems

**Example 1**: Find HCF and LCM of 12, 18, 24 **Solution**: 12 = 2² × 3 18 = 2 × 3² 24 = 2³ × 3 HCF = 2¹ × 3¹ = 6 LCM = 2³ × 3² = 72

**Example 2**: Two numbers have HCF 12 and LCM 180. If one number is 36, find the other. **Solution**: Other number = (12 × 180)/36 = 60
