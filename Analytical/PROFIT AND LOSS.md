Core Formulas

- **Profit** = Selling Price (SP) - Cost Price (CP)
- **Loss** = CP - SP
- **Profit %** = (Profit/CP) × 100
- **Loss %** = (Loss/CP) × 100
- **SP = CP × (100 + Profit%)/100** or **CP × (100 - Loss%)/100**
- **CP = [SP × 100]/(100 + Profit%)** or **[SP × 100]/(100 - Loss%)**

### Marked Price and Discount

- **Discount** = Marked Price (MP) - SP
- **Discount %** = (Discount/MP) × 100
- **SP = MP × (100 - Discount%)/100**

### Important Shortcuts

- If CP of x articles = SP of y articles, then Profit% = [(x-y)/y] × 100
- If a trader professes to sell at CP but uses false weights (w instead of 1kg), Profit% = [(1000-w)/w] × 100
- **Break-even**: When SP = CP (no profit, no loss)

### Successive Discounts

Two successive discounts of a% and b%: Single equivalent discount = [a + b - (ab/100)]%



**Q16.** A merchant has 1,000 kg of sugar, part of which he sells at 8% profit and rest at 18% profit. He gains 14% on the whole. How much did he sell at 18% profit?

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let quantity sold at 18% profit = x kg
Quantity sold at 8% profit = (1000 - x) kg

Using allegation or equation:
8x + 18(1000-x) = 14(1000)
8x + 18000 - 18x = 14000
-10x = -4000
x = 400 kg at 8%

Quantity at 18% = 1000 - 400 = 600 kg

**Answer: 600 kg**

</details>

**Q19.** A man wants 20% profit. He estimates expenses will be 12.5% of selling price. What percent should he mark above cost price?
`like getting ur profit first, then incrementing the price to cover taxes on the profit`
<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let CP = ₹100
Desired SP = ₹120 (for 20% profit)

Expenses = 12.5% of SP = 12.5% of 120 = ₹15

MP must cover: CP + Desired profit + Expenses
MP = 100 + 20 + 15 = ₹135

Markup% = (35/100) × 100 = 35%

**Answer: 35% above CP**

</details>


**Q24.** A trader marks price 50% above CP. He gives successive discounts of 20% and 10%. If he still makes ₹540 profit, find CP.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let CP = x
MP = 1.5x

After 20% discount: 1.5x × 0.8 = 1.2x
After 10% discount: 1.2x × 0.9 = 1.08x

SP = 1.08x
Profit = 1.08x - x = 0.08x = 540
x = 540/0.08 = ₹6,750

**Answer: ₹6,750**

</details>

**Q25.** By selling 33 meters of cloth, a trader gains the selling price of 11 meters. Find profit percentage.

<details> <summary>Solution</summary>

Let SP of 1 meter = ₹1
SP of 33 meters = ₹33
Profit = SP of 11 meters = ₹11

CP of 33 meters = SP - Profit = 33 - 11 = ₹22

Profit% = (11/22) × 100 = 50%

**Answer: 50%**

</details>
