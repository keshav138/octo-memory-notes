### Core Concept

Alligation is used to find the ratio in which two or more ingredients at given prices must be mixed to produce a mixture at a given price.

### Alligation Rule

When two quantities at prices P₁ and P₂ are mixed to get mean price Pm:

Ratio = (P₂ - Pm):(Pm - P₁)

**Visual representation**:

```
        P₁           P₂
         \           /
          \         /
           \       /
              Pm
           /       \
          /         \
     (P₂-Pm)    (Pm-P₁)
```

### Mixture Problems - Key Formulas

- **Removing and replacing**: If 'x' liters from a vessel of capacity 'V' containing liquid A is removed and replaced with liquid B, after n operations:
    - Final quantity of A = V(1 - x/V)^n

### Practice Problems

**Example 1**: Mix milk at ₹50/L with water (free) to get mixture worth ₹40/L. Find ratio. **Solution**: Using alligation: 50 0 \ / 40 /  
40 10 Ratio = 40:10 = 4:1

**Example 2**: A vessel contains 60L of milk. 12L is removed and replaced with water. This is done again. Find final milk quantity. **Solution**: Final milk = 60(1 - 12/60)² = 60 × (4/5)² = 60 × 16/25 = 38.4L
